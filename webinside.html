Know More about ISL

Indian Sign Language (ISL) is a natural, complete language with its own grammar and syntax. It is the primary means of communication for the deaf community in India.

  • ISL is distinct from spoken languages like Hindi or English; it is a visual-gestural language
  • Like other sign languages, ISL has its own unique phonology, morphology, syntax, and semantics.
  • It is not a direct word-for-word translation of a spoken language.
  • ISL has regional variations, similar to dialects in spoken languages
  • It is an integral part of the deaf culture and identity in India.
